Singapore History

This island is named in the 14th century. Legend has it that Prince Sunnyra Utama from Palembang (the capital of Srivijaya) found an animal he had never seen before while out hunting. He names the city "lion city" or "Singapore" at where the animal was found. The name Singapore is from the Sanskrit words "simha" (lion) and "pura" (city). This city situated at the tip of the Malay Peninsula and a natural meeting point of sea routes and once flourished as a trading post for ships such as Chinese sailboats, Arabian dhows, Portuguese battleships and Burginian schooners.

Merlion, Singapore

Merlion, the official moascot of Singapore, depicted as a mythical creature with the head of a lion and the body of a fish. It is a symbol for Singapore. Merlion is widely used to represent the city-state and its people in sports teams, adbertising, bradding, tourism and national personification.The Merlion was originally used as a tourism board logo in Singapore. Its name combines "mer", which means the sea, and "lion". The lion head represents Singapore's original name, Singapura, which means "lion city".

Undoubtedly, food and drink are the key unification for all residents on Little Red Dot, which is why the drab hawker centre is so prominent in Singapore.
